Palworld base location checklist

A Palworld base location checklist for choosing between resources, travel access, terrain, automation, and future expansion.

View planning credits Back to Palworld Lab

Short answer

A good Palworld base is not just pretty or central. It should reduce hauling, support automation, give enough flat space, and fit your next resource bottleneck.

FactorGood signWarning sign
ResourcesNearby materials match your current bottleneck.You constantly travel for basic inputs.
TerrainPals can path and work reliably.Workers get stuck or tasks fail repeatedly.
ExpansionEnough room for production, storage, and defenses.You must rebuild after every upgrade.
TravelUseful access to exploration and boss goals.The base saves no travel time.
SafetyManageable enemy pressure.Frequent interruptions slow production.

Checklist

  • Write down your current resource bottleneck before moving.
  • Check whether Pals can path through the build area.
  • Leave room for production and storage expansion.
  • Do not pick only by scenery.
  • Review base location after major patches that affect pathing or resources.

Example

A scenic cliff base can look great but fail as a production base if workers cannot path cleanly to stations or resource nodes.

Common mistakes

  • Moving before unlocking better transport.
  • Choosing a cramped base because it looks safe.
  • Ignoring pathing and automation reliability.
  • Building far from the resource you need most.
