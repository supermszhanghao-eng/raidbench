Palworld breeding goal checklist

A Palworld breeding goal checklist for deciding what Pal, passive traits, role, and production target you are actually trying to create.

View planning credits Back to Palworld Lab

Short answer

Start breeding with a role goal, not a vague desire for a perfect Pal. Decide whether you need combat power, base work, movement, or a specific production role before spending time on trait combinations.

GoalWhat to defineBad shortcut
CombatBoss target, damage role, survivability.Only chasing rarity.
Base workWork suitability and station role.Using combat traits for production.
MovementTravel route and mount need.Optimizing a mount you rarely use.
CollectionCompletion or personal preference.Pretending every collection goal is efficiency.

Checklist

  • Choose one role for the breeding target.
  • List must-have traits separately from nice-to-have traits.
  • Stop if the target no longer supports your next goal.
  • Keep a simple record of parent lines.
  • Review after balance patches that affect traits or work roles.

Example

A base worker with strong combat traits may look impressive but still be worse than a worker bred for the exact station role.

Common mistakes

  • Breeding without a role target.
  • Mixing combat and work goals too early.
  • Restarting the plan every time a new video appears.
  • Ignoring patch changes to traits or work behavior.
