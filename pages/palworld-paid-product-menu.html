Short answer

The first Palworld paid products should be decision aids, not exploit guides or copied builds. The strongest paid angles are base optimization, boss prep, breeding target planning, and resource/progression routing.

Checklist

  • Keep outputs short and actionable.
  • State the game version and last checked date.
  • Do not sell exploit-dependent methods.
  • Offer a correction if RaidBench made a factual mistake.
  • Use Season Pass for ongoing update coverage.

Example

A player submits base screenshots and current goal. RaidBench returns the one bottleneck, one layout risk, one worker assignment fix, and one next material route.

Common mistakes

  • Selling vague tips that free pages already explain.
  • Promising permanent validity across patches.
  • Charging for bug abuse advice.
  • Building a complex account system before one paid test works.
